### ALTERNATIVE INVESTMENT FUNDS (AIFs) OVERVIEW

* **AIF vs. Mutual Funds**
    * Both are pooled vehicles regulated by SEBI...
    * Key difference: tax treatment, investment scope, liquidity, investor base...
    * Mutual funds: taxes often levied only on withdrawal/redemption (depends on type)...
    * AIFs: often treated as if securities directly owned by investor (pass-through), taxable on portfolio changes/income distribution at investor level...
    * AIFs best for sophisticated investors who don't require immediate liquidity...
* **Credit Fund Categories**
    1.  **Performing Credit Funds (Mosaic's Focus)**
        * Invest in firms with no distress...
        * Good quality management...
        * Returns: 11-14% pre-tax net to investors (typical range)...
        * Lower risk profile (compared to distressed)...
        * Mosaic's Multi-Yield Fund falls in this category...
    2.  **Special Situation Funds**
        * Middle-risk category...
        * Unique situations evolving...
        * Not outright distressed, not purely performing...
    3.  **Distressed Funds**
        * Bet on company turnarounds...
        * Typically companies have already defaulted...
        * Higher risk, higher potential returns (17-18% pre-tax net target)...
* **Investor Choices in Fixed Income**
    * **Liquidity priority**: Mutual fund liquid and liquid-plus funds...
    * **Interest rate bets**: Dynamic bond funds, income funds, medium-term funds...
    * **High yield/Lower Liquidity**: Alternate fund managers (like Mosaic)...
